TEST-ONLY HITL IDEA EXERCISE MODE:
- This mode is enabled only because `NEURICO_HITL_TEST_FORCE_IDEA_MIX` is set.
- During this run, intentionally exercise the checkpoint loop by raising
  multiple ideas while doing real `{{ pipeline_stage }}` work.
- This is a current-run smoke-test contract. Prior memories, old workspace
  commits, old checkpoint logs, and old resolved HITL examples may guide your
  expectations, but they do not count toward this run's required ideas and must
  not be cited as current progress.
- Across the whole resource_finder process, raise at least two evidence ideas
  and at least two decision ideas before completing the stage.
- This is a hard test requirement, not a preference. In this forced mode,
  `.resource_finder_complete` must not be created until `{{ plan_path }}` shows at
  least four resolved raised ideas from execution: two `evidence` ideas and two
  `decision` ideas.
- Use this required execution sequence unless a previous resolved idea in
  `{{ plan_path }}` from this current run already satisfies that slot:
  1. Raise one evidence idea.
  2. After feedback, resume from progress and raise one decision idea.
  3. After feedback, resume from progress and raise a second evidence idea.
  4. After feedback, resume from progress and raise a second decision idea.
- Only one unresolved checkpoint may exist at a time. When a raised idea is
  due for this sequence, write `{{ checkpoint_path }}` and stop. After runtime
  resolves it and resumes you, update `{{ plan_path }}` with the resolution,
  preserve progress, and continue from the next incomplete slot in the sequence.
- The checkpoint filename must be exactly `{{ checkpoint_path }}`. Do not add a
  timestamp, suffix, run id, or any alternate filename; runtime consumes this
  single current-checkpoint path.
- Evidence ideas must use `idea_type: "evidence"` and include an `evidence`
  field, self-contained context, basis, escalation reason, and related artifacts.
  Do not include `decision_needed` or decision options for evidence ideas.
- Decision ideas must use `idea_type: "decision"` and include `decision_needed`,
  substantive options, self-contained context, basis, escalation reason, and
  related artifacts. Put supporting facts in `basis`; do not use a top-level
  `evidence` field as a substitute for `decision_needed`.
- Use the exact checkpoint schema keys from the HITL execution prompt. In
  particular, decision checkpoints must write top-level `context`,
  `decision_needed`, `options`, `basis`, and `reason_for_escalation`. Do not
  substitute alias keys such as `raised_decision`, `options_considered`,
  `explicit_signoff_question`, `blocks`, or `recommendation`.
- Do not write `pipeline_stage` or `hitl_stage` in the checkpoint packet;
  runtime owns those fields.
- Before stopping on a decision checkpoint, inspect the JSON you wrote. If
  `idea_type` is `"decision"` and top-level `decision_needed` is absent, fix the
  packet before stopping; runtime will reject it.
- Keep the ideas research-like and grounded in actual resource-finder work:
  examples include evidence quality, dataset suitability, licensing ambiguity,
  benchmark/source tradeoffs, label/task framing, paper-source reliability, or
  train/dev/test availability.
- Track raised/resolved ideas explicitly in `{{ plan_path }}` so the manager can see
  what has been handled and what remains. Maintain a short forced-test checklist
  in the plan with counts for resolved execution evidence ideas and resolved
  execution decision ideas.
- When the plan or artifacts mention a resolved idea, include enough current-run
  context that the manager can audit it without relying on memory from an old
  run.
