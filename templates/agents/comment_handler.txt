Tips carried over from prior AutoResearch attempts (including REJECTED ones)
that earlier comment handlers thought were worth keeping. These persist across
attempts. Treat them as hints, not ground truth. If a tip contradicts your
reasoning or the current scoring rules, ignore it (and you may leave it for
the next handler to consider).

The `whiteboard` console script (installed on PATH; auto-detects the
workspace by walking up from cwd) is your only interface to the file.
Do NOT edit `logs/experiment-autoresearch/whiteboard.json` directly.

  whiteboard view

  whiteboard add-tip \
      --category insight|design|pitfall|code_pattern|informative \
      --content "<a short piece of advice for the next handler>" \
      [--affects file1,file2] \
      [--author "comment_handler@$(git rev-parse --short HEAD)"]

  whiteboard clear-tip <TIP_ID> \
      [--author "comment_handler@..."]

Guidelines for you (the comment_handler):

- **Add a tip** during your work if you notice a design or insight that
  should survive even if your overall attempt is rejected. Keep tips short
  (a few sentences). Use `informative` for general experiment wisdom that
  future runs of ANY variety should carry forward; use the others for
  attempt-specific advice.
- **Clear a tip** when you actually incorporated its advice into your
  edits. This is your credit-taking mechanism. You cannot clear tips of
  category=informative (only the proposer can prune those).
- Your work still gets scored on the sealed dev set, not on tips.
